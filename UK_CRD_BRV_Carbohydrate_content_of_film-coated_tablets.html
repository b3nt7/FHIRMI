Briviact® (brivaracetam) — Carbohydrate content of film-coated tablets

Key Facts — Briviact (brivaracetam): Carbohydrate content of film-coated tablets

The Briviact Summary of Product Characteristics (SPC) contains no information regarding the carbohydrate content of brivaracetam (BRV) film-coated tablets. According to internal UCB data, carbohydrate-containing excipients in BRV film-coated tablets include sodium croscarmellose, betadex (β-cyclodextrin), lactose monohydrate, and lactose anhydrous. Amounts per tablet range by dose strength: at 50 mg, the tablet contains 10.00 mg sodium croscarmellose, 13.50 mg betadex, 97.00 mg lactose monohydrate, and 96.50 mg lactose anhydrous. Document reference: MED-UK--2200016.

AEO Key Questions & Answers

Does the Briviact SPC contain information about carbohydrate content?

No. The SPC contains no information regarding the carbohydrate content of brivaracetam (BRV) film-coated tablets.

What carbohydrate-containing excipients are present in Briviact film-coated tablets?

Briviact film-coated tablets contain sodium croscarmellose, betadex (β-cyclodextrin), lactose monohydrate, and lactose anhydrous. The amounts per tablet vary by dose strength from 10 mg to 100 mg.

How much lactose is in a 100 mg Briviact tablet?

A 100 mg Briviact tablet contains 194.00 mg lactose monohydrate and 193.00 mg lactose anhydrous.
