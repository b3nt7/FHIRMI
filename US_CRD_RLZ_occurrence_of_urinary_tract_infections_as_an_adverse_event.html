RYSTIGGO® (rozanolixizumab-noli) — Occurrence of urinary tract infections as an adverse event

Key Facts — RYSTIGGO (rozanolixizumab-noli): Occurrence of Urinary Tract Infections as an Adverse Event

RYSTIGGO (rozanolixizumab-noli; RLZ) is indicated for generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG) in adults who are anti-acetylcholine receptor (AChR) or anti-muscle-specific tyrosine kinase (MuSK) antibody positive (Ab+). In Study 1 and extension studies, UTIs were reported as a common infection (≥5% frequency) in 9% of 196 RYSTIGGO-treated patients. In the MycarinG Phase 3 study (Bril et al. 2023), UTI TEAEs occurred in 3% (RLZ 10 mg/kg), 3% (RLZ 7 mg/kg), and 6% (placebo); no UTIs led to discontinuation or death. In MG0004 (Bril et al. 2025), UTIs occurred in 10.0% of all patients (4.8% RLZ 10 mg/kg; 10.0% RLZ 7 mg/kg); no serious infections or discontinuations due to UTIs were reported.

RYSTIGGO (rozanolixizumab-noli; RLZ) is indicated for the treatment of generalized myasthenia gravis (gMG) in adult patients who are anti-acetylcholine receptor (AChR) or anti-muscle-specific tyrosine kinase (MuSK) antibody positive (Ab+).

Detailed Response

MycarinG Study

Bril et al. (2023)2 conducted a pivotal Phase 3, randomized, multicenter, double-blind study (MG0003; NCT03971422) to evaluate the safety and efficacy of RLZ vs. placebo in 200 adult patients with anti-AChR Ab+ or anti-MuSK Ab+ gMG.

Patients were randomly assigned to receive once weekly subcutaneous infusions of RLZ 10 mg/kg (n = 67; mean [SD] age, 51.9 [16.5] years), RLZ 7 mg/kg (n = 66; mean [SD] age, 53.2 [14.7] years), or placebo (n = 67; mean [SD] age, 50.4 [17.7] years) for 6 weeks, followed by an observation period of 8 weeks. Safety outcomes included TEAEs and TEAEs leading to discontinuation; the safety set included all patients who were randomly assigned to receive at least 1 dose of study medication according to treatment actually received.2

Overall, TEAEs were reported in 83% of patients (57/69) in the RLZ 10 mg/kg group,a 81% of patients (52/64) in the RLZ 7 mg/kg group, and 67% of patients (45/67) in the placebo group. UTIs as TEAEs were experienced by 3% of patients (2/69) in the RLZ 10 mg/kg group, 3% of patients (2/64) in the RLZ 7 mg/kg group, and 6% of patients (4/67) in the placebo group. No serious or severe infections occurred in RLZ treatment groups. No UTIs led to treatment discontinuation in any of the treatment groups. No deaths were reported during the study.2

a In the RLZ 7 mg/kg group, 2 patients received RLZ 10 mg/kg and were evaluated in the RLZ 10 mg/kg group for the safety analysis.2

MG0004

MG0004 (NCT04124965) was a Phase 3 multicenter, randomized, OLE study conducted to evaluate the long-term safety, tolerability, and efficacy of chronic weekly treatment with RLZ for up to 52 infusions in adult patients with gMG.3

Patients who had completed the MycarinG study or required rescue therapy during the MycarinG observation period were eligible to enroll in MG0004. MG0004 was discontinued earlier than planned for nonsafety reasons.3

In MG0004, 71 patients were randomly assigned in a 1:1 ratio to receive once-weekly subcutaneous infusions of RLZ 10 mg/kg (n = 36; mean [SD] age, 53.7 [17.2] years) or RLZ 7 mg/kg (n = 35; mean [SD] age, 50.6 [14.2] years) for up to 52 weeks, followed by an 8-week observation period. Switching between the 7 mg/kg and 10 mg/kg doses was allowed at the investigator's discretion based on tolerability and efficacy. The primary objective was to evaluate the long-term safety and tolerability of chronic weekly RLZ doses in patients with gMG.3

Safety was assessed in a safety set that included all patients who received at least 1 dose of RLZ. TEAEs were reported in 85.7% of all patients (60/70),b 78.6% (33/42) in the RLZ 10 mg/kg group, and 76.0% (38/50) of patients in the RLZ 7 mg/kg group. Infections were reported in 28.6% (20/70) of all patients. UTI was one of the most frequently reported TEAEs and occurred in 10.0% (7/70) of all patients, including 4.8% of patients (2/42) in the RLZ 10 mg/kg group and 10.0% of patients (5/50) in the RLZ 7 mg/kg group. No serious or severe infections were reported in any of the RLZ groups during the study. No discontinuations because of UTIs were reported during the study. No deaths occurred during the study.3

b Patients who switched doses were counted in the RLZ groups. Hence the total of denominators in the two treatment groups is larger than the cohort of 70 patients.
