Bimzelx® ▼ (bimekizumab) — Comparison to risankizumab in patients with psoriatic arthritis

Key Facts — Bimzelx® ▼ (bimekizumab): Comparison to risankizumab in patients with psoriatic arthritis

The bimekizumab (BKZ) Summary of Product Characteristics (SPC) contains no information on the comparison of BKZ with risankizumab (RZB) in patients with psoriatic arthritis (PsA). BE BOLD (NCT06624228) is an ongoing Phase 3b, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, active-controlled, parallel-group head-to-head study in adult patients with active PsA. A total of 553 patients were randomized 1:1 to BKZ or RZB. The primary endpoint is ACR50 response at Week 16. Topline data show BKZ was statistically significantly superior to RZB in ACR50 at Week 16, with no new safety signals identified through Week 16. Document reference: MED-UK-BKZ-2600012.

Published Data

BE BOLD (NCT06624228), an ongoing, Phase 3b, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, active-controlled, parallel-group study, is a head-to-head study designed to compare the efficacy and safety of BKZ and the interleukin (IL)-23 inhibitor RZB in adult patients with active PsA.2,3

To be eligible for inclusion, patients must be ≥ 18 years of age with a diagnosis of active adult-onset PsA, as classified by the Classification Criteria for Psoriatic Arthritis (CASPAR), for ≥ 6 months prior to screening. In addition, patients must be currently receiving or have previously received conventional systemic therapy,2,a must have symptoms of active PsA and a baseline tender joint count ≥ 3 out of 68 and a swollen joint count ≥ 3 out of 66, must have ≥ 1 active psoriatic lesion(s) and/or a history of chronic plaque psoriasis, and must have tested negative for rheumatoid factor and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies. Patients are excluded if they have been diagnosed with inflammatory conditions other than psoriasis or PsA, have active symptomatic Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis at screening or baseline (a diagnosis without active symptoms was permitted), or have current or prior exposure to biologics, including BKZ or RZB, except for 1 prior TNF inhibitor.2,b The study design is presented in Figure 1.2

a Patients are permitted to continue their background medication for the entire duration of the study.

b Patients who previously received a TNF inhibitor and experienced an inadequate response to treatment administered at an approved dose for ≥ 3 months or had intolerance of the treatment.

Figure 1 — BE BOLD Study Design (Adapted).a,2

a This is a planned study protocol that may be revised prior to the start of the study.

b BKZ 320 mg Q4W through Week 16, followed by BKZ 320 mg Q8W thereafter.

c RZB 150 mg administered at baseline, Week 4, and Q12W thereafter.

ACR50 = American College of Rheumatology criteria ≥ 50% response; BKZ = bimekizumab; QXW = every X weeks; RZB = risankizumab.

The primary endpoint is achievement of American College of Rheumatology 50 (ACR50) response at Week 16.c Secondary and exploratory endpoints include minimal disease activitye at Week 16; a composite endpoint of ACR50 and Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI) 100f response at Week 16;g ACR50 response at Week 4; clinical efficacy outcomes across the domains of PsA; patient-reported outcomes; health-related quality of life outcomes; and work productivity outcomes.d Safety outcomes include the incidence of treatment-emergent adverse events, serious adverse events, and treatment-emergent adverse events leading to discontinuation of the study drug.2

c Defined as ≥ 50% improvement in the ACR response criteria.

d Additional exploratory endpoints other than those mentioned here may also be included in the study.

e Defined as the achievement of ≥ 5/7 of the following criteria: tender joint count ≤ 1; swollen joint count ≤ 1; either PASI ≤ 1 or a body surface area ≤ 3% affected with psoriasis; patient's assessment of arthritis pain (visual analogue scale) ≤ 15; Patient's Global Assessment–PsA visual analogue scale ≤ 20; Health Assessment Questionnaire Disability Index ≤ 0.5; Leeds Enthesitis Index ≤ 1.

f Defined as 100% improvement from baseline in PASI score.

g In patients with psoriasis that affected ≥ 3% of body surface area at baseline.

ACR50 = American College of Rheumatology criteria ≥ 50% response; BKZ = bimekizumab; IL = interleukin; PASI = Psoriasis Area and Severity Index; PsA = psoriatic arthritis; Q4W = every 4 weeks; Q8W = every 8 weeks; Q12W = every 12 weeks; RZB = risankizumab; TNF = tumour necrosis factor.

A total of 684 patients enrolled in the study, of whom 553 were randomized. The study started on 21 Oct 2024, and the estimated completion date is 26 May 2026.3 Topline data from BE BOLD show that BKZ has demonstrated statistically significant superiority vs. RZB in achieving an ACR50 response at Week 16. BKZ was generally well tolerated through Week 16, with no new safety signals identified. UCB plans to submit the full BE BOLD results to a forthcoming international congress.4
